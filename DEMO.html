On the matter of seeing

An equirectangular photograph is a sphere unrolled — latitude down the vertical axis, longitude along the horizontal, a strict two-to-one aspect. This demo reverses the unrolling: the image is pasted to the inside of a sphere, the observer is placed at its centre, and a pointer may drag the gaze in any direction. The world, as it were, folds back around you.

i.

Inverted Sphere

A 500-unit SphereGeometry has its normals flipped so the panorama faces the camera at its centre. The sphere becomes a room.

ii.

Spherical Drag

Pointer deltas update longitude and latitude. Drag speed scales with the field-of-view, so a telephoto view pans more slowly — as it should.

iii.

Zoom by Wheel

The scroll-wheel drives FOV between twenty-five and one-hundred degrees. Latitude is clamped at the poles to prevent vertigo.

// 1. an inside-out sphere, textured with the panorama
const geo = new THREE.SphereGeometry(500, 64, 40);
geo.scale(-1, 1, 1);  // flip normals — texture on the inside

const tex = new THREE.TextureLoader().load("panorama.jpg");
tex.colorSpace = THREE.SRGBColorSpace;

scene.add(new THREE.Mesh(geo, new THREE.MeshBasicMaterial({ map: tex })));

// 2. aim the camera at a point on the sphere derived from lon/lat
const phi   = THREE.MathUtils.degToRad(90 - lat);
const theta = THREE.MathUtils.degToRad(lon);
camera.lookAt(
  500 * Math.sin(phi) * Math.cos(theta),
  500 * Math.cos(phi),
  500 * Math.sin(phi) * Math.sin(theta)
);
